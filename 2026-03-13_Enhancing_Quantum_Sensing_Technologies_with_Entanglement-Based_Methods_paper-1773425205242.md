However, the sensitivity of these systems is often limited by the inherent noise characteristics of the measurement process.

Recent advances in quantum information processing have demonstrated the potential for entanglement-based methods to enhance sensitivity in quantum sensing applications. Entangled states, characterized by strong correlations between the system and environment, can be exploited to generate highly correlated sensor outputs. This leads to improved accuracy and precision in sensing applications.
