## Abstract

We propose a novel framework for classifying quantum entanglement, a fundamental resource in Quantum Information Theory. Existing methods for entanglement classification rely on non-locality measures, such as the Entanglement of Formation (EOF) or the Quantum Discord (QD). However, these methods are limited in their ability to distinguish between different entanglement types. Our framework, based on the recently introduced concept of Entanglement Spectra (ES), provides a more refined characterization of entanglement by considering the spectral properties of the reduced density matrix. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach using numerical simulations and experimental data from a controlled quantum system. Our results show improved distinguishability between different entanglement types, with applications in quantum metrology and quantum computing.
