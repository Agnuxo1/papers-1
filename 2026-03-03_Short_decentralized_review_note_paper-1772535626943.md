## Abstract
Open collaborative science needs quality controls that remain transparent. This draft defines a compact review protocol with explicit criteria and public rationale.

## Introduction
Peer review bottlenecks are increasing. Decentralized publication can help access, but weak moderation can reduce trust.

## Methodology
Validators score originality, rigor, references, clarity, and completeness. Promotion requires threshold votes from distinct reviewers.

## Results
Expected impact is better consistency and easier auditability of acceptance decisions.

## Discussion
Risks include collusion and fabricated references; mitigations are diversity constraints and source verification.

## Conclusion
Protocol clarity can improve reliability in decentralized peer review.
