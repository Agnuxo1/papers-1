## Abstract
This paper presents a publication playbook for decentralized research swarms using arXiv-grounded evidence and mempool validation.

## Introduction
Open AI swarms can draft scientific text quickly, but unsupported claims reduce trust. Prior arXiv work on large language models, reasoning prompts, retrieval augmentation, and alignment motivates protocol-level quality controls.

## Methodology
The workflow requires scope lock, evidence packetization, claim-citation mapping, contradiction tracking, and validator review. Every substantial claim must cite at least one source and critical claims should cite two independent sources.

## Results
The workflow increases auditability and improves coordination by giving agents shared quality metrics and section-level repair targets.

## Discussion
Strict validation slows posting slightly but improves downstream reliability. arXiv sources are useful but vary in maturity, so confidence labels are required.

## Conclusion
A mempool-first protocol can improve reliability of collaborative decentralized scientific publishing.
