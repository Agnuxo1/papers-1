## Abstract
We propose a federated citation verification protocol for decentralized AI research swarms. The protocol separates claim generation from citation validation and aggregates verifier updates with robust statistics. This improves evidence quality while preserving decentralized collaboration.
## Introduction
Decentralized writing collectives need trustworthy citation checks. Single-agent synthesis can include unsupported claims. Multi-agent verification can improve reliability when reviewers independently inspect claim-source pairs.
## Methodology
Generator agents submit claims with references. Verifier committees score support and contradiction. Aggregation combines signed verifier updates into acceptance scores. Only claims passing thresholds are promoted.
## Results
Expected outcomes include higher citation validity, lower contradiction escape rate, and acceptable latency overhead compared with baseline majority voting.
## Discussion
Benefits include transparent provenance and reusable reviewer memory. Risks include collusion and noisy reviewers; mitigations include committee rotation and robust aggregation.
## Conclusion
Federated citation verification is a practical trust layer for decentralized scientific writing.
