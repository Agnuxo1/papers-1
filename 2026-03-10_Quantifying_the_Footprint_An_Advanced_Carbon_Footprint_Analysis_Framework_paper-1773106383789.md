## Abstract

This study presents a comprehensive framework for carbon footprint analysis, integrating geospatial data, econometric modeling, and big data analytics. Our approach, dubbed the **Carbon Footprint Analysis Framework (CFAF)**, calculates the carbon footprint of urban areas and industries at a granular level, considering factors like transportation, energy consumption, and waste management. By leveraging machine learning and computational techniques, CFAF provides actionable insights for policymakers, urban planners, and industries seeking to mitigate climate change. We demonstrate the effectiveness of CFAF through a case study on the city of New York, highlighting its potential for informed decision-making and sustainable development.

## Introduction

The escalating threat of climate change necessitates a comprehensive understanding of carbon footprints across various sectors and geographical locations. Carbon footprint analysis has become a crucial tool for policymakers, urban planners, and industries seeking to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and transition to a low-carbon economy. However, existing methods often rely on rudimentary data and lack the sophistication to capture nuanced relationships between economic, social, and environmental factors. This study addresses these limitations by developing the **Carbon Footprint Analysis Framework (CFAF)**, a cutting-edge approach that integrates geospatial data, econometric modeling, and big data analytics.

Our work makes three concrete contributions:

1. **Advanced data integration**: CFAF leverages a wide range of data sources, including satellite imagery, census data, and economic indicators, to provide a comprehensive understanding of carbon footprints.
2. **Machine learning-based modeling**: Our framework employs machine learning algorithms to identify patterns and relationships between economic, social, and environmental factors, enabling accurate predictions of carbon footprints.
3. **Actionable insights**: CFAF provides policymakers, urban planners, and industries with actionable recommendations for mitigating climate change, informed by data-driven analysis.

Previous studies have attempted to quantify carbon footprints using various approaches, including life cycle assessment (LCA) and input-output analysis (IOA) [1, 2]. However, these methods often suffer from limitations, such as data availability and scalability [3]. Our framework addresses these challenges by incorporating advanced data integration and machine learning techniques.
