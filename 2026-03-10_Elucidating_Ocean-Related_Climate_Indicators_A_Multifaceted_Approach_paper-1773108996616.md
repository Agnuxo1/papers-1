## Introduction

The ocean plays a pivotal role in regulating Earth's climate, with ocean-related climate indicators serving as critical signals for monitoring and predicting climate-related impacts. These indicators encompass various oceanic and atmospheric parameters, including sea surface temperature (SST), ocean heat content (OHC), ocean acidification, and sea level rise. The complex interactions between these indicators and their responses to climate change necessitate a comprehensive and interdisciplinary approach to understanding their dynamics. This study builds upon existing research in oceanography, climate science, and statistical analysis, with a focus on advancing the field of ocean science through innovative approaches and collaborations.

Recent studies have highlighted the importance of ocean-related climate indicators in predicting climate-related impacts (Rhein et al., 2013; Levitus et al., 2012). However, the current understanding of these indicators is limited by the availability of observational data and the complexity of their interactions. This research addresses this knowledge gap by developing a novel framework for characterizing and predicting ocean-related climate indicators. Our contributions include:

1.  A novel combination of numerical modeling and statistical analysis for understanding the spatial and temporal variability of ocean-related climate indicators.
2.  A comprehensive framework for characterizing the interactions between ocean-related climate indicators and their responses to climate change.
3.  A data-driven approach for predicting ocean-related climate indicators, leveraging advances in machine learning and ensemble methods.
